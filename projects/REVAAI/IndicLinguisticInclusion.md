# 🇮🇳 Indic Linguistic Inclusion: Solving for the 90%

A critical theme in Dr. Manish Gupta's RAISE'25 keynote is the **"English Barrier."** While AI development is globally centered on English, only 10% of India's population is proficient in it. To be truly impactful, AI must speak the language of the other 90%.

## 🗣️ The Challenge of Diversity
India possesses one of the world's most complex linguistic landscapes:
- **700+ Districts**: Each with unique linguistic identities.
- **Thousands of Dialects**: Many of which are strictly oral with no written script.
- **Low-Resource Status**: Most Indian languages lack the massive text corpora required to train standard LLMs.

## 🎙️ Project Vaani (formerly Project Vani)
**Project Vaani** is a collaboration between Google, the Indian Institute of Science (IISc), and ARTPARK to map this diversity.
- **Objective**: Collect and open-source an India-representative, multimodal speech dataset.
- **Methodology**: Using image-based prompts to trigger spontaneous, natural speech in native dialects across all districts.
- **Scale**: The project has released 14,000+ hours of localized speech data to help developers build inclusive models.

## 💰 Karya: Ethical Data Ingestion
**[[Karya]]** is an innovative social enterprise that partners in this data collection effort.
- **Model**: Rural workers are paid fair, high-market wages to provide voice data and annotations via smartphones.
- **Virtuous Cycle**: 
    1. Local communities generate data in their native tongue.
    2. They receive direct economic benefit (income).
    3. The resulting AI models are optimized for their specific regional needs (agriculture, health).

## 🚀 Impact Areas
1. **Linguistic Equity**: Enabling voice-based transactions and information access for non-English speakers.
2. **Education**: AI-powered tutors that explain complex concepts in local dialects.
3. **Governance**: Making public services accessible via natural language interfaces.
