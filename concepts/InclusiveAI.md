# 🌍 Inclusive AI

Inclusive AI refers to the development of artificial intelligence systems that are accessible, equitable, and beneficial to all populations, regardless of leur linguistic, socioeconomic, or geographic background.

## Key Focus Areas in India
As highlighted by Dr. Manish Gupta (Google DeepMind) in his RAISE'25 talk, Inclusive AI in India focuses heavily on:

### 🗣️ Linguistic Diversity
- **The English Barrier**: Only 10% of India's population is proficient in English, while most AI models are trained on English datasets.
- **Dialect Mapping**: Mapping the 1.4 billion people across 700+ districts who speak thousands of dialects.
- **[[Project Vani]]**: A large-scale effort to collect and map linguistic data from across India.
- **[[Project Vaani]]**: An open-source initiative providing 14,000+ hours of localized speech data to developers.

### 💰 Economic Inclusion
- **Data Work for Rural India**: Platforms like [[Karya]] provide fair-wage work for data annotation to rural communities, creating a virtuous cycle where local data improves AI for the people who generated it.

### 🏥 Social Impact
- **Localized Solutions**: AI applied to local challenges in agriculture, primary healthcare, and native-language education.
