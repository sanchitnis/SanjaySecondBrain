# 🔬 AI for Science

AI for Science is an emerging paradigm where artificial intelligence is used not just as a tool for computation, but as a primary driver of scientific discovery and hypothesis generation.

## Breakthrough Examples
Dr. Manish Gupta underscores that AI is now driving Nobel-prize winning research:

### 🧬 Biology: [[AlphaFold]]
- **The Problem**: Predicting how a protein's 1D sequence folds into a 3D structure (the "protein folding problem").
- **The AI Solution**: AlphaFold achieved experimental ground-truth accuracy, effectively "solving" a 50-year-old challenge in biology.
- **Impact**: Accelerating drug discovery and understanding of life at a molecular level.

### 🌦️ Meteorology: [[GraphCast]]
- **Capability**: AI-based weather forecasting.
- **Performance**: Outperforms traditional numerical models (which rely on complex differential equations) in terms of both speed and predictive accuracy for extreme weather events.

### 🛠️ Scientific Foundation Models
- Models trained on vast scientific datasets (chemical properties, genomic sequences, climate data) to predict outcomes that were previously only possible through slow, expensive physical experiments.
